Communities

I would have never found out about all of these glitches if I hadn't found websites that explained how they work and why they happen in detail. There are many different groups of people who try and find glitches and exploits in games as a hobby, and over time, I've become familiar with a few of them. Here are some from the Pokémon community:

Glitch City Laboratories

Glitch City Laboratories was a forum and wiki that catalogued glitches and other oddities people had found in the Pokémon games. They had an extensive number of pages on glitch Pokémon in particular. If you were to ever go looking for information about Pokémon glitches on a different website, you would find that their source would link back here.

Recently, there was a massive Nintendo leak, and all the source code for the first four generations of Pokémon games was made public. The owners of Glitch City Laboratories realized that they could no longer prove that new edits didn't come from people who were poking around in the source code, so they shut down to avoid being targeted by Nintendo's legal team. However, you can still find an archived version of the website.

Bulbapedia

Bulbapedia is a general resource for Pokémon-related information. You can find everything there from what a specific Pokémon trading card looks like to what happened in an obscure manga. They have a whole section of their website dedicated to some of the more common glitches found in the Pokémon games.

PRAMA Initiative

PRAMA Initiative is a French website similar to Glitch City Laboratories that has a catalogue of information about Pokémon glitches. I know a lot less about this website because it's not in English, but I did go through a phase where I would try to use my basic knowledge of French to try and translate everything and understand what they were talking about. Because of this, I do know that they have a more in-depth explanation of how some of the bigger glitches in the games work than other websites.
